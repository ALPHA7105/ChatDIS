# SCHOOL CLUBS FOR GRADE 6 UPWARDS
### Aim:
#### School clubs are instrumental in nurturing well-rounded individuals by enhancing academic knowledge, developing essential skills, fostering social bonds, and encouraging creativity
### Objectives:
- **Academic Enhancement:** Clubs aim to supplement formal education by providing a platform for students to delve deeper into specific subjects or fields of interest.
- **Skill Development:** Clubs serve as incubators for honing various skills such as leadership, communication, teamwork, and critical thinking, contributing to well-rounded personalities.
- **Social Engagement:** Facilitating camaraderie and inclusivity, clubs create a sense of belonging, promoting positive social interactions among students with shared interests.
- **Creativity and Innovation:** Encouraging out-of-the-box thinking, clubs stimulate creativity and innovation by providing a space for students to explore, experiment, and express their ideas.
### Functions:
- **Regular Meetings:** Clubs hold regular meetings to discuss, plan, and execute activities related to their focus area, ensuring sustained engagement and progress. Meetings may be held during or after school hours.
- **Workshops and Events:** Organizing workshops, seminars, and events helps members acquire new knowledge, skills, and experiences, contributing to their personal and academic growth.
- **Collaboration:** Clubs often collaborate with each other, fostering a cross-disciplinary approach and promoting a sense of unity within the school community.
- **Community Outreach:** Many clubs engage in community service or outreach programs, instilling a sense of responsibility and empathy among students towards societal needs.

# DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
#### Students are given appropriate guidance and opportunities to identify the motivation and purposes behind their actions and to rectify any negative behaviors before any disciplinary action (e.g. Warnings, written notices) is taken.
#### Student misconduct shall be dealt which as follows:
- ➢ Firstly , the school provides counselling for the students, with a clear explanation , with reasons, of the changes in behavior that are required of the student by the school.
- ➢ Next, the school has put in place a strategy, with the appropriate monitoring and support by the teacher and school counsellor to address and correct the student’s unacceptable behavior.
- ➢ If there is a need for further escalation of response, the school will inform parents/guardians by letter and hold a meeting or a series of meetings with there to agree to a reasonable joint home school strategy. Parents/guardians shall be required, at this stage, to sign an undertaking to support the agreed strategy.
- ➢ Should the student continue to behave unacceptably, the school may suspend the student temporarily from school for up to five days and shall issue to the student and his or her parent /guardian a final warning.
- ➢ In the final stage, if the student fails to modify his/her behavior in accordance with the requirements of the school. The school may apply to the council to transfer the student to another School or to permanently exclude the student concerned.
